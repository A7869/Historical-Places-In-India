HISTORICAL PLACES IN INDIA

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the south bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan (reigned from 1628 to 1658), to house the tomb of his favourite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

The Red Fort (Lal Qila) is a historic fort in Delhi, India that was the main residence of the Mughal emperors. It's a UNESCO World Heritage site and a symbol of India's national pride.

Read more

India Gate is a war memorial in New Delhi that honors Indian soldiers who died in World War I and the Afghan War. It's located near the Rajpath (now called Kartavya Path).

Read more

The Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi, India. It's a red sandstone tower that was built in the 12th century and is the tallest masonry tower in India.
